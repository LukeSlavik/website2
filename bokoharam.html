Facts about terrorist group "Boko Haram"

  • Became active in 2003
  • The name Boko Haram means "Western education is sinful"
  • Aimed at establishing an Islamic Caliphate in north-east Nigeria
  • In 2014 they kidnapped 276 schoolgirls from a secondary school
  • Boko Haram controlled land the Size of Belgium at the start of last year
  • About 2,000 boys and girls have been kidnapped from the beginning of 2014
  • Boko Haram used 44 children to carry out suicide attacks in West Africa last year
  • The group has forced around 2-4 million people to flee from their homes in the countries of Chad, Niger, Nigeria, and Cameroon.
  • The militant group has killed more than 15,000 people
  • Boko Haram pledged its allegiance to the Islamic State

What steps are they taking to help?

The United States for the third time in three years are moving towards selling 12 Super Tucano attack planes. The Obama Administration stopped the proposed sale, two years ago, after a Nigerian fighter jet looking for members of Boko Haram accidentally dropped bombs on refugees, killing some and wounding others. Last year in February Trump had a conversation with the president of Nigeria saying he would take a look at the proposal again. The White House made a statement that President Trump shows support for the sale to happen to support Nigeria's fight against Boko Haram. officials from the state department, the pentagon, and The White House said the sale of the attack planes would be valued at $600 million, and would help the Nigerian Air Force battle Boko Haram.

How are they staying out of it?

The United States for the most part are staying out of the fight against Boko Haram. "And the U.S. under President Barack Obama has been reluctant to intervene where it believes America does not have obvious security concerns". The United States have not been very willing to join the fight against the cutthroats of Nigeria. One reason Western powers including the U.S. are staying out of it, is because these African terrorists across the ocean do not directly threaten them. The United States has not sent troops to Nigeria showing they want to keep their military personnel away from the insurgency of Boko Haram. Overall the United States are giving little help to their allies of Nigeria and aren't willing to put their troops on the ground but they do give them support in other ways.
